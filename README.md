## 🧠 Quality Fundamentals & Strategy

[cite_start]As a **Certified Tester (ISTQB®)** [cite: 9, 10][cite_start], my approach is built on a solid theoretical foundation that ensures efficiency and risk mitigation throughout the SDLC[cite: 5, 8].

<details>
<summary><b>Click to expand: The 7 Fundamental Principles of Testing</b></summary>

[cite_start]These principles guide my decision-making process when planning and executing comprehensive tests[cite: 21, 31]:

1. **Testing shows the presence of defects, not their absence:** Our goal is to reduce the probability of undiscovered defects. A clean test run doesn't mean the software is 100% bug-free.
2. [cite_start]**Exhaustive testing is impossible:** I use risk analysis and prioritization to focus efforts on what matters most for project success[cite: 31, 32].
3. [cite_start]**Early testing (Shift-Left):** We start testing as early as possible—validating user stories and requirements—to find defects when they are cheaper to fix[cite: 27].
4. **Defect clustering:** Experience shows that a small number of modules usually contain most defects. I target deep-dive sessions in these critical areas.
5. [cite_start]**Pesticide paradox:** I regularly review and update test cases and documentation to ensure they remain effective in identifying new issues[cite: 22].
6. [cite_start]**Testing is context-dependent:** The testing approach is adapted based on the specific needs of the application and stakeholders[cite: 37].
7. [cite_start]**Absence-of-errors fallacy:** Fixing bugs is irrelevant if the system fails to fulfill user needs and expectations[cite: 27]. I focus on delivering high-quality software solutions that align with end-user requirements.

</details>
